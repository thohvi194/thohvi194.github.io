This is a sample of some of the work I've completed over the course of my undergraduate career. It includes class projects as well as personal projects.

Procedural Story Generation Algorithm

  • Algorithm that uses Monte Carlo Tree Search to create stories that conform to two different models of story structure. Stories are also evaluated on 'believability' and how many user defined goals they accomplish.
  • Semester-long-senior project completed in accordance with Latin honors requirements at the University of Minnesota.
  • Extension of previous work completed by Bilal Kartal at the Univeristy of Minnesota.
  • Conducted under the supervision of Bilal Kartal and Dr. Stephen J. Guy.
  • Paper available here.

"Plagiarized in Plain Sight": Unity Clone of Hidden in Plain Sight with Improved Crowd AI

  • Group project that recreated Hidden in Plain Sight, a multiplayer game focused on blending into a crowd of AI agents.
  • The AI crowd moved randomly in the original game. We recreated the game, and modeled the crowd as agents acting under the influence of Dr. Stephen Guy’s empirical Pedestrian Universal Power Law, which produces more realistic movement.
  • Game also features hand-made animations, controller support, and full sound.
  • Group Members: Josh Ford, Thomas Hvizdos, Devin Lange, and Sam Vipond.
  • Sourced Assets:
    • Unity Game Engine
    • Soundtrack : texasradiofish
    • Lamppost : http://www.turbosquid.com/3d-models/street-lamp-3d-model/473402
    • Ground texture : http://opengameart.org/content/stone-pavement-ground-tile
    • Normal Map Creation : http://www.crazybump.com/

FlashPhoto: A Middle-Weight Image Editing Application

  • Pair of image editing applications:
    • FlashPhoto: A program similar to MSPaint, with additional features
    • MIA: A simpler "medical image editing" application with a command line interface.
  • Features include:
    • Tools: Pen, Spray Can, Calligraphy Pen, 'Grass' brush, etc.
    • Filters: Blur, Threshold, Quantize, Pixelize, Edge-detect, etc.
    • Image save/load: JPG and PNG supported.
    • Mia program features a command line interface that allowed for applying filters to many images at once with one command.
  • Group project for the UMN software design class.
  • Extensive documentation and images available here

FIRETEAM: A Squad Level Tactical Turn-Based Shooter

  • Written from the ground up in C++ and SFML. Levels created in Tiled and stored as XML.
  • Turn based shooter featuring directional cover, hand-to-hand and ranged combat, resource management, and 5 unique squad members.
  • Visual-novel-style narrative, providing story choices that impact game path, combat encounters, supplies, and other variables.
  • GUI featuring context sensitive highlighting and hover-over text.
  • Written over summer 2015, while also working 20-30 hours a week at a part time job
  • Download

Note: FIRETEAM is mechanically complete, and playable. However, time constraints prevented full implementation of the content I had originally planned for the game. The unfinished “demo” portion has 4 levels, more than 5500 lines of text, and 60+ pieces of art. A recent hard drive crash (and lack of backups) recently rendered the source code, writing, and art unrecoverable.

Note 2: I went into this project with no knowledge of C++, game design, or software design principles. While I’m proud of the results from a technical standpoint, the game was pretty evidently overambitious, and I’m not sure it’s very much fun to play if you’re not me. However, it was an extremely valuable practical lesson in the importance of playtesting, clean and modular design, and the amount of effort that goes into creating a finished product.
